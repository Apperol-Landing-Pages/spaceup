Privacy Policy for the SpaceUp App

1. Introduction

Welcome to SpaceUp, a utility application created to help Apple device users keep their digital space organized, secure, and optimized. This Privacy Policy explains how SpaceUp handles information when you use the application, what data may be processed, and how your privacy is protected.

SpaceUp was designed with a strong commitment to user privacy and transparency. The application focuses on providing tools that help users identify unnecessary files, organize media libraries, detect duplicate contacts, review calendar entries, and improve device efficiency. Because the app interacts with certain areas of your device — such as photos, contacts, and calendar data — it is important that you understand how this information is handled.

SpaceUp is intended exclusively for Apple ecosystem users and is compatible with iOS devices. The application may be available in multiple regional versions and localized for users in Europe, the United States, Japan, and South America.

By installing or using SpaceUp, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy.

2. Scope of This Policy

This Privacy Policy applies to all versions of the SpaceUp mobile application distributed through official Apple platforms. It governs how information is handled when users interact with the app’s features, including but not limited to:

  • Media cleaning and duplicate detection
  • Calendar event management
  • Contact analysis and merging
  • Security Center features
  • Ad-blocking tools
  • Battery optimization recommendations
  • Email leakage checking
  • System protection status monitoring

This policy applies regardless of the country in which the app is used, although local laws may provide additional rights depending on your region.

3. Philosophy of Data Minimization

SpaceUp was built according to the principle of data minimization. This means the application attempts to process only the information strictly necessary for its functionality.

In most cases, SpaceUp performs operations locally on the user’s device. Local processing ensures that sensitive data — such as photos, contacts, or calendar events — does not leave the device unless explicitly required for a specific service and authorized by the user.

Our goal is to provide effective cleaning and optimization tools without collecting or storing personal data unnecessarily.

4. Permissions and Device Data Access

To provide its core functionality, SpaceUp may request permission to access certain areas of your iOS device. These permissions are optional and can be revoked at any time through your device settings.

Photos and Media Library

If the user chooses to analyze their photo or video library, the application may access the media database to:

  • Identify duplicate photos or videos
  • Detect visually similar images
  • Locate large or unnecessary files
  • Provide media cleanup recommendations

All analysis is performed on-device whenever possible. SpaceUp does not upload or store your personal photos on external servers.

Contacts

When using the Contact Cleaner feature, SpaceUp may access your contacts to:

  • Detect duplicate entries
  • Identify incomplete or empty contacts
  • Suggest merging similar records
  • Provide deletion recommendations

Contact processing is conducted locally on the device, and no contact data is stored by SpaceUp servers.

Calendar

If you enable the calendar cleaning tool, the application may scan calendar events to:

  • Identify outdated events
  • Detect duplicated entries
  • Suggest removal of unnecessary records

SpaceUp does not permanently store or transmit calendar event data.

5. Security Center Functionality

SpaceUp includes a Security Center designed to provide users with helpful insights regarding device protection and system status.

The Security Center may analyze:

  • iOS version and update availability
  • System security recommendations
  • Protection status indicators
  • Privacy and security suggestions

This information is used solely to provide guidance to the user about maintaining device security and ensuring that their operating system remains updated.

SpaceUp does not collect sensitive system diagnostics beyond what is required to display these recommendations.

6. Ad Blocking Feature

The application may include an optional Ad Block feature intended to reduce intrusive advertising in supported browsing contexts.

If enabled, the ad-blocking functionality may utilize filtering lists and network rules to prevent certain advertising domains from loading. This process operates through configuration mechanisms supported by the iOS platform.

SpaceUp does not monitor personal browsing activity, collect browsing histories, or track websites visited by the user.

7. Email Leakage Monitoring

SpaceUp provides a feature that allows users to check whether their email address may be associated with known marketing lists or public database exposures.

When a user voluntarily enters an email address into this tool, the address may be temporarily processed to compare it against publicly available data breach or marketing distribution records.

This feature is designed to:

  • Inform users about potential email exposure
  • Alert them if their address appears in known mailing databases
  • Encourage better privacy practices

Email addresses entered into this feature are not stored by SpaceUp beyond the processing required for the check.

8. Battery Optimization Recommendations

SpaceUp includes educational tools and guidance intended to help users improve battery performance.

The battery optimization section may provide:

  • Tips for reducing background activity
  • Guidance on adjusting system settings
  • Recommendations for efficient charging habits
  • Suggestions for improving device longevity

These recommendations are informational and do not require the collection of personal usage data.

9. Trial Access and Subscription Services

SpaceUp may provide a trial period that allows users to explore premium features before purchasing a subscription.

During the trial period:

  • Users may gain temporary access to advanced cleaning and security tools
  • No personal content is collected differently from standard usage
  • Billing and subscription management are handled through Apple’s App Store infrastructure

SpaceUp does not directly store payment information. All financial transactions are processed by Apple according to their policies.

10. Regional Availability and Localization

SpaceUp is distributed globally and includes localization options for users in multiple regions, including:

  • European countries
  • The United States
  • Japan
  • South America

Localization may affect language display, legal notices, and certain feature explanations. However, the fundamental privacy practices remain consistent across all regions.

Where applicable, SpaceUp strives to comply with international privacy regulations, including data protection standards relevant to each region.

11. Data Storage and Security

Although most processing occurs locally on the device, any limited data interactions that may occur are protected using industry-standard security practices.

  • encrypted network communication
  • secure server infrastructure
  • restricted internal access to technical systems

SpaceUp is designed to avoid unnecessary storage of personal information.

12. Children’s Privacy

SpaceUp is intended for general audiences but is not specifically designed for children under the age of 13.

The application does not knowingly collect personal information from children. If it becomes known that such information has been provided unintentionally, steps will be taken to remove it promptly.

Parents or guardians who believe a child may have provided information through the app may contact support.

13. User Control and Permissions

Users maintain full control over the permissions granted to SpaceUp.

At any time, users may:

  • revoke access to photos, contacts, or calendar data
  • uninstall the application
  • disable optional features
  • withdraw permission through iOS settings

Disabling permissions may limit the functionality of certain tools but will not affect other parts of the application.

14. Changes to This Privacy Policy

From time to time, this Privacy Policy may be updated to reflect improvements to the application, regulatory changes, or new features.

When significant updates occur, the revised version will include a new “Last updated” date and may be presented to users within the application.

Continued use of SpaceUp after changes indicates acceptance of the updated policy.

15. Contact and Support

If you have questions regarding this Privacy Policy or the privacy practices of SpaceUp, you may contact the application support team through the official support channel provided within the app listing or settings section.

Support requests are handled in accordance with applicable privacy and data protection principles.

Summary

SpaceUp was built to help Apple users manage digital clutter, optimize device performance, and improve privacy awareness. The application prioritizes local processing, minimal data handling, and transparent communication about how information is used.

Your privacy and control over your personal data remain central to how SpaceUp operates.
